# Complete the Look One-Click Bundle (No Discount)

**Context:** You sell foundation, beauty sponges, and setting spray on Shopify. You want a convenience-only bundle (no discount)—one click to add all three together.

---

## 1. Bundle naming

Use names that signal “full routine” and “one click,” not “sale” or “deal”:

- **Primary:** **Complete the Look** or **Complete the Look Bundle**
- **Short (buttons/CTAs):** **Add Complete Look** or **Add Full Routine**
- **Subcopy:** *Foundation + sponge + setting spray in one click*

Optional variants if you A/B test:

- **Full Face Essentials**
- **One-Click Complete Look**
- **Foundation Routine Bundle**

Avoid: “Starter Kit,” “Value Bundle,” “Save Now”—they imply discount.

---

## 2. Where to put it on the product page

**Best placement:** **Below the main Add to Cart, above or within the product description.**

- **Option A (recommended):** A clear block/section under the primary CTA:
  - Heading: **Complete the Look**
  - One line: *Add foundation + sponge + setting spray together.*
  - Single button: **Add Complete Look** (or **Add Full Routine**)
- **Option B:** A compact line under Add to Cart: *Or add the full routine: [Add Complete Look].*
- **Option C:** In the product description as a highlighted box (same copy + one button).

**Show the bundle on all three product types** (foundation PDP, sponge PDP, spray PDP) so customers can add the full set from any entry point. Keep copy identical so it’s recognisable.

**Avoid:** Putting it only in cart/upsell apps (you want it on PDP for one-click intent) or burying it in footer/FAQ.

---

## 3. What to measure

Track these so you know if the bundle is used and whether it helps without discounting.

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Bundle add-to-cart rate** | % of PDP visitors who click “Add Complete Look.” Baseline for adoption. |
| **Bundle conversion rate** | % of those who add the bundle who then checkout (bundle in cart). |
| **Basket composition** | When the bundle is in cart, do they keep all 3 SKUs or remove items? (If they strip items, placement or naming may be off.) |
| **AOV with vs without bundle** | Compare AOV when the bundle is in the order vs when it isn’t. Confirms value of convenience. |
| **Units per order (foundation / sponge / spray)** | Before vs after bundle. Expect more 1 foundation + 1 sponge + 1 spray orders. |
| **Bounce/exit on PDP** | If you add the bundle and bounce goes down, the option may be holding interest. |

**Implementation notes for measurement:**

- Use one **cart attribute or line-item property** (e.g. `bundle: complete_the_look`) or a dedicated **bundle product/SKU** so you can segment “bundle orders” in Shopify Reports or your analytics.
- In Google Analytics (or similar), fire an event when “Add Complete Look” is clicked (e.g. `add_to_cart` with a bundle parameter) so you can report **bundle add-to-cart rate** and **bundle conversion rate** in one place.

**Success looks like:** Higher add-to-cart of the bundle over time, stable or improved AOV on bundle orders, and more orders containing all three products—without any discount.

---

## Summary

- **Naming:** “Complete the Look” / “Add Complete Look” (and “Full Face Essentials” if you test).
- **Placement:** On-PDP block under main Add to Cart on foundation, sponge, and setting spray pages; one clear CTA.
- **Measurement:** Bundle add-to-cart rate, bundle conversion rate, AOV with vs without bundle, units per order, and (optionally) PDP bounce—all identifiable via a bundle tag or dedicated bundle product.
