# Full Base Kit Bundle: Structure, PDP Copy & Tracking

## 1. How to Structure the Bundles

### Option A: Single “Full Base Kit” bundle (recommended to start)
- **Contents:** 1 primer + 1 concealer + 1 powder + 1 brush set (or 1–2 key brushes).
- **Pricing:** Set a bundle price that’s ~10% less than the sum of individual SKUs (e.g. $120 list → $108 bundle).
- **Mechanics:** One fixed SKU (“Full Base Kit”) so checkout is simple and inventory is one bundle unit.

### Option B: “Build your base” (pick components)
- Customer chooses primer, concealer, powder (and optionally brush set) from dropdowns; 10% off applies at cart when all required items are present.
- **Pros:** More choice, can upsell premium items. **Cons:** More complex (rules, validation, copy).

**Recommendation:** Launch with **Option A**. If it performs, add Option B later as “Custom Base Kit.”

### What to include in the fixed kit
- **Primer** (1): bestseller or hero product.
- **Concealer** (1): one shade or “most popular” shade + link to full shade range for add‑on.
- **Powder** (1): universal or bestseller.
- **Brushes:** either a small “base brush set” (e.g. 2–3 pieces: foundation, concealer, powder) or one multi-use brush to keep the kit simple and cost-controlled.

### 10% discount
- Apply at **bundle level** (bundle price = sum of components − 10%), not as a code. Reduces code abuse and keeps messaging clear: “Full Base Kit — 10% off when you buy the set.”

## 2. PDP Copy for the Full Base Kit

### Headline
- **Primary:** “Full Base Kit — Everything You Need for a Flawless Base”
- **Subhead:** “Primer, concealer, powder + brushes. One set, 10% off.”

### Value proposition (short paragraph)
- “Get your complete base routine in one box: smoothing primer, buildable concealer, and setting powder, plus the brushes to apply them like a pro. Buy the set and save 10% compared to buying each piece separately.”

### Bullets (for PDP and meta)
- Complete base routine in one kit: primer, concealer, powder + brushes.
- Save 10% when you buy the set (vs. buying each item alone).
- Perfect for beginners or anyone who wants a no-fuss base.
- Everything you need for a smooth, even finish—no guesswork.

### Social proof
- “X customers bought the Full Base Kit this month” (if you have data).
- Or: “Rated 4.X by customers who bought the Full Base Kit” / “Most popular bundle.”

### CTA
- Primary: **“Add Full Base Kit to Cart — Save 10%”**
- Secondary: “See what’s included” (expandable or link to a “What’s in the kit” section).

### “What’s in the kit” section
- List each product with thumbnails and one-line benefit (e.g. “Primer — blurs pores, extends wear”).
- Show **individual prices** and **bundle price** with the 10% saving highlighted (e.g. “$120 value → $108 when you buy the kit”).

### Trust / urgency (optional)
- “Free shipping on orders over $X” or “Ships in 1–2 business days.”
- “Limited-time bundle price” only if it’s true; otherwise avoid fake urgency.

### SEO / discoverability
- Title tag: “Full Base Kit — Primer, Concealer, Powder & Brushes | [Brand]”
- Meta description: Include “full base kit,” “primer, concealer, powder,” “10% off,” and one benefit (e.g. “everything you need for a flawless base”).

## 3. How to Track If It’s Working

### Main metrics
- **Bundle units sold** (and % of total order volume).
- **Bundle revenue** and **bundle AOV** (revenue / number of bundle orders).
- **Conversion rate:** PDP views (or “add to cart” clicks) → bundle purchases.
- **Attach rate:** % of customers who land on the bundle PDP and buy the bundle vs. buying a single item or leaving.

### Attribution
- **UTM / source:** Tag links and campaigns with `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign` (e.g. `full_base_kit_10pct`).
- **Segment in analytics:** Create a segment or report for “orders containing Full Base Kit SKU” to get revenue, AOV, and conversion from bundle traffic.

### What “working” looks like
- **Lift in AOV:** Bundle orders have higher AOV than non-bundle (you’re selling 4 items instead of 1).
- **Conversion:** Bundle PDP converts at or above your site average (or at least doesn’t drag it down).
- **Margin:** After the 10% discount, margin is still acceptable; if not, consider a smaller discount or a slightly higher bundle price.

### Simple tests
- **A/B test:** Bundle PDP vs. sending traffic to a “shop all base” category (measure conversion and revenue per visitor).
- **Copy test:** “Save 10%” vs. “Complete base routine in one box” as primary message (measure add-to-cart and purchase rate).
- **Promo placement:** Test showing the bundle on product pages for primer, concealer, or powder (“Complete your base — add the Full Base Kit and save 10%”).

### Reporting cadence
- **Weekly:** Bundle units, bundle revenue, conversion rate for bundle PDP.
- **Monthly:** Bundle as % of base-category revenue, AOV comparison (bundle vs. non-bundle), and margin after 10% off.

---

## Summary

- **Structure:** One fixed “Full Base Kit” (primer + concealer + powder + brushes), 10% off baked into the bundle price.
- **PDP copy:** Clear headline and “10% off the set,” bullets and “What’s in the kit” with value and savings, strong CTA and optional social proof.
- **Tracking:** Bundle units, revenue, AOV, conversion rate, UTM and segment by bundle SKU; run small PDP and copy tests and review weekly/monthly to see if the bundle is lifting AOV and margin.
