# High-Ticket Trust & Conversion

Build trust and shorten the decision cycle for stores selling expensive, considered-purchase products—so browsers become inquiries, and inquiries become buyers.

## Who this skill serves

Stores selling **high-AOV, long-decision** products where trust is the primary conversion barrier:

- **Jewelry & fine accessories** — custom rings, luxury watches, gemstone pieces
- **Art & collectibles** — original artwork, limited editions, high-end prints
- **Premium home & furniture** — designer furniture, custom cabinetry, premium lighting
- **Large appliances & equipment** — commercial-grade machines, ergonomic setups, pro A/V
- **Medical beauty & wellness** — aesthetic treatments, premium skincare devices, cosmetic procedures
- **Education & high-value services** — bootcamps, coaching programs, consulting packages

The common thread: buyers need convincing evidence before committing hundreds or thousands of dollars, and the sale often involves multiple touches, comparisons, and sometimes family or group decisions.

## When to use this skill

Trigger on any of these signals—even if the user doesn't say "trust" or "high-ticket" explicitly:

- "My landing page gets traffic but nobody converts"
- "Customers ask questions but never buy"
- "How do I handle price objections?"
- "People add to cart but abandon before paying"
- High AOV mentioned (>$200, or context suggests expensive products)
- Long decision cycle (days to weeks between first visit and purchase)
- Lead capture, consultation booking, or quote-request flow design
- PDP / landing page optimization for premium or luxury products
- Sales or CS script writing for considered purchases
- Reducing returns, cancellations, or post-purchase regret
- Improving close rate, quote acceptance, or inquiry-to-sale conversion
- "Nobody trusts my brand" or "how do I look more credible"

## Scope (when not to force-fit)

- **Low-AOV impulse items** (snacks, small accessories, cheap tools) — use a high-repeat skill like `high-repeat-small-goods-ops` instead; trust-building ROI is low when the purchase risk is trivial.
- **Checkout flow optimization** (payment methods, shipping options, cart UX) — this skill focuses on pre-checkout trust and persuasion, not checkout mechanics.
- **Pure B2B enterprise deals** (RFPs, tenders, large custom projects) — reuse the trust asset audit and decision-path diagnosis, but shift emphasis from online conversion to lead quality and offline relationship.
- **Single-deliverable requests** ("write one DM reply" / "one popup line") — use a light version: quick diagnosis + that deliverable, skip the full 8-module plan.

When the scenario doesn't fit, explain why, then point out which modules can still be reused.

## First 90 seconds: get the key facts

Extract answers from context first; only ask what's missing. Keep to 6–8 questions:

1. **Category & price band** — What do you sell? Rough AOV? Any higher-tier bundles or upgrades?
2. **Decision cycle & context** — How long from first touch to purchase? Common scenarios (self-use, gift, family decision, group buy)?
3. **Main acquisition channels** — Search, social, ads, offline-to-online, owned lists? Where does first contact happen (homepage, landing, PDP, form, DM)?
4. **Leads & conversion today** — Rough inquiry-to-close rate? Where do you lose people (bounce, cart abandon, no reply, drop at negotiation)?
5. **Existing trust assets** — Certifications, test reports, brand story, factory/showroom, case studies, press, celebrity/KOL clients?
6. **Delivery & post-purchase** — Lead times, shipping/install, warranty/returns, issue handling? Any "above expectation" service?
7. **Primary goal this round** — More inquiries/leads? Higher close rate? Higher AOV? Lower refund/cancel?
8. **Tech & resource constraints** — Can you edit pages/add components? Sales/CS team size? CRM or automation tools?

If the user shares links, screenshots, or copy: diagnose from those first, then ask only the 2–3 missing pieces.

## Output structure

Every response includes at minimum a **Summary** and an **Action list for this cycle (14–30 days)**. For full system plans, use all 8 modules below.

### 1. Summary (3–5 key points)

- **Trust & conversion stage**: Cold start / early trust / good reputation but low conversion / strong referral but weak new-visitor conversion.
- **Top 3 gaps**: Be specific—"landing hero lacks authority signals," not "need more trust."
- **Priority actions (next 30 days)**: 3–5 moves that can shift metrics within one month.
- **Target metrics**: Inquiry rate, lead rate, add-to-cart, quote acceptance, close rate, refund rate, NPS, referral rate.

### 2. Decision-path diagnosis

Map the actual buyer journey—not an abstract funnel. Example:

> Awareness → first visit → save/follow/wishlist → inquire/request quote → multi-touch comparison → decide & pay → delivery → post-purchase & referral

For each step, output:
- **Buyer's mental state**: What they're thinking, fearing, or needing proof of at this moment.
- **Problem hypothesis**: 1–2 specific hypotheses based on user input and category norms.
- **Evidence needed**: Which pages, data, or conversations to examine for validation.

Specificity matters—"authority evidence missing on PDP" is useful; "increase trust" is not.

### 3. Trust asset audit

Produce a checklist covering:
- **Brand & credentials** — History, awards, certifications, test reports, physical presence.
- **Expertise & process** — Team background, methodology, case studies (before/after, measurable results).
- **Delivery & risk** — Production steps, QC, delay/damage/dissatisfaction handling.
- **Price & value** — Pricing rationale, comparison to cheaper alternatives, long-term TCO.
- **Social proof** — Verified reviews, client interviews, video testimonials, referral stories.

For each category: what exists, what's missing (ranked by conversion impact), and what can be created within 30 days.

### 4. Page & content trust design

For the main conversion page (home, landing, PDP, booking form), produce a redesign checklist:

- **Above-the-fold** — Does it answer "who / what / for whom / why it's worth the price" in 5 seconds?
- **Verifiable evidence** — Can users click, zoom, or verify claims—not just read "certified"?
- **Transparency** — Materials, process, options, scope, and "what we don't do" clearly stated?
- **Price framing** — How price is shown (range/package/custom), "why this price," payment security.
- **Risk reversal** — Returns, warranty, remake policy stated upfront—not buried in footer?
- **Comparison** — Vs cheaper or common alternatives, with clear reasoning?
- **Form/inquiry UX** — Minimal fields, clear "what happens next," privacy and opt-out visible?

Include rough module order and, when copy or screenshots are shared, section-by-section markup with replacement ideas.

### 5. Inquiry & sales/CS SOP

High-ticket sales almost always need multiple human touches. Output a communication SOP:

1. **First touch** — Confirm need and budget without interrogation; show expertise in 1–2 lines.
2. **Offer / quote** — Break down price (materials, time, design, service, guarantee); steer comparison toward long-term value and hidden costs of cheap alternatives.
3. **Objection handling** — Common objections (price, fear of failure, fear of being sold, family disagreement) with structured reply templates: empathize → name the risk → evidence/case → options.
4. **Close** — "Small commitment → big commitment" (e.g. book assessment first, then deposit); create a decision window through real constraints (slots, schedule, materials), not artificial pressure.

Include a copy examples table (scenario | customer line | recommended reply) and a workflow diagram (inquiry → close, who does what, where it's logged).

### 6. Lead nurture rhythm (7–30 days)

Output an X-day nurture schedule:
- **Touchpoints**: 24h, 3d, 7d, 15d, 30d after first inquiry.
- **Goal per touch**: Fill info gap / answer new question / share case / remind deadline / invite experience.
- **Content ideas**: Real process shots, before/after, client story, FAQ, team intro.
- **Channels**: Pick 1–2 per scenario (email, SMS, WhatsApp, WeChat, call).

When CRM or automation exists, add segments (high intent / hesitant / silent / churned) with tags and triggers.

### 7. Metrics & validation

Two levels:
- **Outcome metrics**: Leads, closes, GMV, AOV, refund/cancel rate.
- **Process trust metrics**: Time on page, inquiry rate, quote acceptance, multi-touch completion, NPS, referral intent.

Tie each key action to a metric and observation window:
- "Replace landing hero + add trust block → watch inquiry rate and hero bounce for 14 days"
- "Add client-story block → track whether inquiries mention it"

### 8. Execution schedule & ownership

Output a 2–4 week plan. Use `scripts/generate_execution_plan.py` to scaffold, then fill in details:
- **Weekly theme & goal** (e.g. "build trust assets," "redesign inquiry flow," "launch nurture sequence")
- **Actions this week** (page edits, asset creation, copy training, system config)
- **Owner & estimated hours** (for solo operators, help them prioritize)

## Output style

- **Conclusion first, then detail** — lead with "what to do first," expand below.
- **Everything actionable** — steps, lists, tables; theory only to explain the "why" behind a recommendation.
- **No anxiety** — frame around "more confident decisions," not "you're losing money."
- **Honest about uncertainty** — label assumptions and flag where validation data is needed before committing.

For narrow asks, use a light version: summary + the relevant module + 2–3 concrete changes. Don't overload a focused request with a full 8-module plan.
